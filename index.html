Course Overview

Description: This course provides a comprehensive introduction to modern deep learning, covering both fundamental principles and state-of-the-art models. Topics include deep neural network foundations (MLPs, optimization, initialization), regularization techniques, convolutional neural networks, graph neural networks, sequence modeling architectures (RNNs, LSTMs, Transformers and recent alternatives), multi-modal foundation models, deep reinforcement learning, self-supervised representation learning, and deep generative models such as GANs, VAEs, and diffusion models. The course emphasizes both theoretical understanding and practical insights, with applications spanning computer vision, natural language processing, and multi-modal learning.

Pre-requisites: There are no formal pre-requisites for this course. However, students are strongly recommended to have prior exposure to basic machine learning concepts and Python programming. Familiarity with undergraduate-level mathematics, including calculus and probability, will be helpful for understanding the course material.

Note: This course is designed for senior undergraduate and graduate students. The content progresses from foundational deep learning concepts to advanced modern architectures and research-oriented topics. Students are expected to engage actively with both theoretical discussions and practical implementations.

    Collaboration Policy

    • All submitted work must represent your own independent effort. You are welcome to discuss ideas, concepts, and approaches with other students, tutors, and lecturers.
    • However, you must not copy, share, or distribute complete or near-complete solutions, nor ask others to verify the correctness of your answers, whether in person or via online platforms (e.g., Ed or Canvas).
    • If you discuss an assignment with anyone other than course staff, you must clearly acknowledge their names at the beginning of your submission.

    AI Assistants Policy

    • The rules governing the use of AI assistants (e.g., ChatGPT and similar tools) are the same as those for seeking help from other people.
    • As this is a deep learning course, students are encouraged to explore and experiment with modern AI tools in order to better understand their capabilities and limitations. Developing this understanding is part of the learning objectives of the course.
    • You may use AI assistants to ask conceptual questions, clarify lecture material, or receive general guidanceâ€”just as you would when consulting course staff during office hours.
    • However, you must not use AI assistants to generate complete or substantial portions of your assessed work, in the same way that you would not ask another person to complete an assignment on your behalf.
    • If you are unsure whether a particular use of an AI tool is appropriate, consider whether the same request would be acceptable if made to a human.
    • If you make use of any AI tools while working on an assignment, you must briefly disclose which tools were used and how they were used at the beginning of your submission.

    Attendance Policy

    • Class attendance is optional. Lecture recordings will be made available on Canvas shortly after each session for students who are unable to attend in person.

    Late Policy

    Suppose you submit an assessment after the deadline and have not been granted special consideration or alternative arrangements:

    • A penalty of 5% of the maximum mark will be applied for each day (or part of a day) late.
    • After 10 days past the deadline, a mark of zero will be awarded.
    • Late submissions are rounded up to full days; for example, submitting 1 hour late counts as 1 late day.

    Examples:

    • If an assignment is worth 40% of the final mark and is submitted 1 hour late, the maximum achievable mark will be 38%.
    • If an assignment is worth 40% of the final mark and is submitted 28 hours late, the maximum achievable mark will be 36%.

    Warning: Submission systems may become very slow close to deadlines. Students are strongly encouraged to submit early. You may resubmit your work if time permits before the deadline.
